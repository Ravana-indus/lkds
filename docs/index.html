One nation.
One design language.

LankaDS is the national design system of the Government of Sri Lanka — the shared foundation behind Sewa, the national super app, and every government digital service. Its philosophy is fixed: easy access · inclusivity · citizen first. The system leads with Unity Gold — the one colour of the flag that belongs to every community — and turns the rising pillars into tokens, components and patterns, so every ministry ships services that feel like one modern, progressive, trusted government.

Foundations

Design principles

The philosophy is fixed: easy access · inclusivity · citizen first. Six commitments flow from it, and they resolve disputes — when two options conflict, the higher principle wins.

Citizen first, ministry second පුරවැසියා පළමුවகுடிமக்கள் முதலில்

Organise around life events — a birth, a job, a business, a pension — never around departmental structure. The citizen should finish a task without learning how government is organised.

Inclusive by design සියලු දෙනාටමஅனைவரையும் உள்ளடக்கி

No community, language, age or ability is an edge case. The interface leads with Unity Gold — the flag's shared colour — because even a palette can take sides. Trilingual parity, assisted modes and equal pillar status are enforced at token level, not left to goodwill.

Easy access for everyone පහසු ප්‍රවේශයஎளிதான அணுகல்

WCAG 2.2 AA is the floor, not the ceiling. 48 px targets, plain language, journeys a first-time smartphone user finishes unaided — and assisted use at a Grama Niladhari office designed in from the start.

Trilingual by default භාෂා තුනෙන්මமும்மொழிகளிலும்

Sinhala, Tamil and English are designed together, not translated after. Every screen, error and SMS ships in all three; layouts are tested against the longest of the three strings.

Built for every connection සෑම සම්බන්ධතාවයකටමஎல்லா இணைப்புகளுக்கும்

Honour the 2G village and the budget Android. Pages under 500 KB, offline-tolerant flows, SMS/USSD fallbacks for critical notifications.

Progressive, modern, trusted නවීන හා විශ්වාසනීයநவீனமும் நம்பிக்கையும்

Government services should feel as good as the best consumer apps — fast, warm, beautifully made. The same button, words and receipt everywhere is also a security feature: when every legitimate screen looks the same, phishing stands out.

Brand & logo

The Sewa mark is a skyline of pillars in the four colours of the national flag — communities at different points in their journey, rising together toward a shared golden summit. It reads simultaneously as a city rising, a chart climbing, and a flame.

The mark

Sewa
සේවා சேவை

Symbolism

  • Four colours, one flag. Maroon, saffron, gold and green carry the communities of the national flag into a single rising form — held in deliberate equality.
  • Unequal heights. Citizens start from different places; the system exists to help every pillar rise.
  • The golden summit. Gold is the colour the flag gives to everyone — the border that wraps all communities equally. That is why it leads the interface: every primary action a citizen takes is golden.
  • The forward lean. Pillars shear 6° forward: progress, not monument. A government in motion.

The mark shown here is an illustrative CSS reconstruction. Production use must always start from the official vector asset files held by the GovTech brand office.

The national ribbon

A 6–8 px strip of the four colours marks the top of every official page and document — the fastest possible signal of authenticity. Order is fixed (green → saffron → maroon → gold) and must never be re-sequenced.

token: --lk-gradient-ribbon — fixed order, fixed proportions (25% each)

Misuse

Do
Use official assets at fixed proportions, on approved backgrounds, with full clear space. Pair the wordmark with Sinhala සේවා and Tamil சேவை lockups where space allows.
Don't
Recolour the pillars, re-order them, rotate or mirror the mark, add shadows or outlines, stretch it, place it over busy photography, or rebuild it per ministry.
Foundations

Colour

All colour is sampled from the Sewa mark itself, then extended into eleven-step ramps. Unity Gold leads; the four pillars hold equal categorical status; warm stone neutrals do the listening. Every approved text pairing meets WCAG 2.2 AA — most reach AAA.

Why gold leads — inclusion, enforced in the palette

In the national flag, maroon, saffron and green each belong to one community. Gold belongs to all of them — it is the border that wraps every panel equally, and the shared summit of the Sewa mark. A state interface that led with any single community's colour would quietly take a side. LankaDS therefore gives the lead role to gold, and uses the community pillars only as equal categorical accents. Inclusivity here is not a value statement — it is enforced in the tokens.

Three hard rules from the maths1 · Gold never carries white or light text — every gold surface takes ink (9.54:1 on gold-500). 2 · Saffron-600 may carry headlines and graphics, never body text — step down to saffron-700+. 3 · The focus ring is ink on light surfaces and gold-500 on dark surfaces — gold alone on light fails 3:1.

Usage proportion

A government screen is calm paper with deliberate colour. Target proportions: ~80% stone neutrals & white · ~12% unity gold actions and highlights · ~8% pillars as equal category accents. No single community pillar may visually dominate a screen — if a layout starts reading maroon, saffron or green, rebalance it. The full-strength pillar palette appears together only in the brand mark and the national ribbon.

Rules for three scripts

Line height by script

Sinhala carries stacked diacritics (combuva, papilla, hal kirīma) above and below the baseline; Tamil ligatures also run tall. Body leading: Latin 1.6 · Sinhala 1.75 · Tamil 1.7. Apply via :lang(si) / :lang(ta) — never clip ascenders in fixed-height containers.

No capitals, no italics

Sinhala and Tamil have no case and no italic tradition. Never use all-caps styling or synthetic italics on localised strings. For emphasis use weight (600/700) or colour. Latin overlines/letter-spacing tricks must be disabled when the string is si/ta.

Length expansion

Sinhala and Tamil strings run 20–40% longer than English. Design every button, tab and nav label against the longest of the three translations. Truncation of an official term is a defect, not a compromise.

Numerals, dates, currency

Use Latin digits across all three locales (standard practice in Sri Lankan government publishing). Currency: Rs. 1,250.00 (LKR). Dates: 2026-06-13 in data, "13 June 2026 · 2026 ජූනි 13 · 13 ஜூன் 2026" in prose.

Language switcher — the signature control

Present on every screen, top-right (web) or in profile + onboarding (app). Always renders each language in its own script — never "Sinhala, Tamil" in English. Switching is instant, never resets the journey, and persists across all government properties. The active language sits on a gold pill with ink text — the citizen's chosen language is literally golden. Compact form only where space is genuinely constrained.

Patterns

Repeatable journeys assembled from components. If a ministry's flow matches one of these, it must use the pattern — divergence requires a design-council exception.

The service journey

Every transactional service follows the same five-act arc, mapped to the stepper: Start page → Identity → Form (one topic per page) → Review & payment → Confirmation.

ActRequirements
1 · Start pageWhat you'll get, what you'll need (documents listed up front), fee, processing time. One primary button: "Apply now". Citizens should never discover a missing document at step 4.
2 · IdentitySign in once via the national digital ID; never re-collect what government already knows ("once-only" principle). Assisted mode lets a Grama Niladhari officer act on a citizen's behalf, visibly logged.
3 · FormOne topic per page · autosave · plain-language questions · resume from SMS link on any device.
4 · Review & payFull answer summary with per-item edit links. Fees itemised before payment. Government receipt number issued instantly.
5 · ConfirmationGreen confirmation panel, reference number in large type, what happens next + expected date, download/SMS the receipt. Status then trackable in "My applications".

Errors & empty states

Error pages take responsibility ("We could not load your documents"), offer a retry, a path back home, and the 1919 Government Information Center line for human help. Empty states explain what will appear and offer the first action — never a blank panel.

Standards

Content & language

The voice of Sewa: a capable officer who respects your time. Warm, direct, never bureaucratic, never cute. The state speaks plainly because clarity is a form of respect.

Voice rules

  • Address the citizen as "you"; government is "we". Active voice: "We will post your certificate" not "The certificate will be dispatched".
  • Verbs first on actions: "Apply now", "Download receipt", "Track application".
  • No abbreviations a first-time user wouldn't know; expand on first use (Grama Niladhari (GN)).
  • Honest about time: "Usually 3 working days" beats silence; if delayed, say so and why.

Trilingual parity

  • All three languages are authored together at the same release — Sinhala and Tamil are never "translation debt".
  • Terminology follows the Department of Official Languages glossary; new coinages are ratified before release.
  • Translations are reviewed by native speakers in context (on the screen), not in a spreadsheet.
  • Proper nouns (Sewa, place names) keep native script forms beside Latin where space allows.

Design tokens

Tokens are the single source of truth, named in three layers so a ministry can re-theme nothing and rebuild everything: core (raw values) → semantic (meaning) → component (application).

/* layer 1 — core: the fact */
--lk-gold-500: #F0B41E;

/* layer 2 — semantic: the meaning (what teams use) */
--lk-color-action-primary: var(--lk-gold-500);
--lk-color-action-on-primary: var(--lk-ink); /* never white on gold */

/* layer 3 — component: the application */
--lk-button-primary-bg: var(--lk-color-action-primary);

/* scripts get correct metrics via lang attributes */
:lang(si) { font-family: var(--lk-font-sinhala); line-height: 1.75; }

Both files accompany this document. Naming convention: --lk-{category}-{concept}-{variant}. Teams build against semantic tokens only; core values may be tuned centrally without breaking products. Dark theme ships as [data-lk-theme="dark"] — app-first, opt-in on web.

Standards

Governance & roadmap

A design system is a product with users — its users are ministry teams. It needs ownership, versioning and a front door.

MechanismHow it works
OwnershipA core systems team inside GovTech owns tokens, components and this documentation; a cross-ministry design council reviews proposals monthly
VersioningSemantic versioning. Patch = fixes · minor = additive components · major = breaking token changes with 6-month migration windows. Nothing breaks a live citizen service unannounced
ContributionAny ministry may propose a component with evidence of need in ≥ 2 services; the core team generalises, names, documents, tests, and ships it for everyone
CompliancePre-release service assessment: token usage, WCAG 2.2 AA audit, trilingual review, page-weight budget, status-vocabulary check
DistributionFigma libraries, npm web components, Flutter/Compose/SwiftUI packages — all generated from the same token source
